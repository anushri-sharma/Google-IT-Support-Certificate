# Use generative AI to help you work smarter and faster

## 🔥 AI benefits for IT support: automates routine tasks, speeds work, and enables higher‑impact effort

– **Reduces time spent on repetitive requests**, freeing IT professionals for higher‑level initiatives.

– **Shifts work from reactive firefighting to proactive problem solving**, improving support quality and organizational value.

– Practical uses include **troubleshooting issues** and **enhancing customer service**.

## 🛠️ Prompting framework: T‑C‑R‑E‑I (Task, Context, References, Evaluate, Iterate) and mnemonic "Thoughtfully Create Really Excellent Inputs"

<img width="521" height="518" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/7e308ab6-6365-4f47-bea7-3f007c4284e9" />

1. **Task**: specify what you want the model to do; include persona and format.
  
  – **Persona** examples: a professional speech writer; a marketing executive with 15 years of experience; output tailored for a specific audience (customer, manager).
  
  – **Format** examples: bulleted list, short sentence, table.

2. **Context**: supply necessary details so the model understands constraints and goals.
  
  – Example contrast: vague request "Give me ideas for a birthday present under $30" versus detailed request "Give me five ideas for a birthday present. My budget is $30. The gift is for a 29‑year‑old who loves winter sports and has recently switched from snowboarding to skiing."
  
  – Note: more specific context yields more useful, targeted outputs.

3. **References**: optionally provide examples or prior outputs the model should use or avoid.
  
  – Example: listing previously given gifts helps the model suggest new, relevant ideas.
  
  – Caveat: references may be unavailable for abstract/ideation tasks.

4. **Evaluate**: check whether the output meets your needs; assess accuracy, relevance, and safety.

5. **Iterate**: refine the prompt by adding details or edits if the output is insufficient; iteration is essential to effective prompting.
  
  – Order of steps is flexible; **substance over sequence**.

## ✍️ Prompting best practices and phrasing guidance

– Use **natural language** as if speaking to a person and express complete thoughts.

– Be **clear and specific** about desired outcome, audience, constraints, and format.

– Include persona and format details when relevant and be as detailed as needed.

– Iteration and refinement are expected parts of the workflow.

## 🔎 Human‑in‑the‑loop and responsible AI use

– Always apply a **human‑in‑the‑loop** approach: combine human expertise with model outputs to train, verify, and refine results.

– **Verify and validate** all AI outputs; models lack the depth of practical experience, judgment, and interactive skills of humans.

– Be mindful before entering **confidential or sensitive information**; consult organizational policies first.

– Avoid entering personal/confidential data into AI tools outside work and **check how input data will be used or stored.**

## 🧭 Tools and transferability of techniques

– Demonstration used **Gemini**, but the techniques and best practices apply to other generative AI tools such as **ChatGPT, Copilot, and Claude**.

– Prompt structure, iteration, and verification are broadly applicable across models.

## ⚖️ Practical implications for IT workflows and service delivery

– Using AI to handle repetitive tasks **increases bandwidth** for engineers to focus on complex problems and user empowerment.

– Enhances the ability to **deliver greater value** across the organization by reallocating time to proactive improvements and user support.
