
# [What's a post-mortem?](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_z9bXurICCs&t=14s)


# Notes

 **Summary on the Nature and Importance of Mistakes**  
- Mistakes are an inherent part of being human and occur regularly.  
- Each mistake offers a **learning opportunity**: by analysing what happened, individuals or teams can understand the causes and avoid repeating the same errors.  
- The analogy of a toddler touching a hot stove illustrates the natural process of learning through experience and pain: a single negative consequence leads to lasting behavioural change.  
- Without this learning process, we would continuously repeat harmful actions, akin to repeatedly burning ourselves.  
- This fundamental concept underpins the purpose of a **post-mortem report**.

**Definition and Purpose of a Post-Mortem Report**  
- A post-mortem is created after an incident, outage, or event where something has gone wrong, or after project completion.  
- It is a detailed documentation that captures:  
  - **What happened before, during, and after the event or project**.  
  - **What went well and what did not**.  
- The key objective is to **learn from the experience**, rather than to assign blame or punish individuals.  
- While mistakes identified are addressed, the focus is on understanding root causes to prevent recurrence, not shaming.

**Post-Mortem Process and Communication**  
- Writing the report is only part of the process; **effective communication of the report is essential**.  
- The value of post-mortems extends beyond the immediate team involved in the incident or project.  
- Sharing these reports across other teams within an organisation:  
  - Promotes a **culture of transparency and learning**.  
  - Demonstrates a willingness to acknowledge errors openly, which can act as a positive example for others.  
  - May help other teams identify similar vulnerabilities or problems in their own infrastructure or processes.  
- This cross-team sharing can reveal areas that require improvement even if those areas are outside the original team's responsibility.  
- Hence, the phrase **“sharing is caring”** encapsulates the collaborative benefit of distributing post-mortem insights.

**Cultural Impact of Embracing Post-Mortems**  
- Organisations that embrace post-mortems foster a culture where making mistakes is considered acceptable and part of growth.  
- A **healthy attitude towards mistakes** reduces fear of failure.  
- Without this attitude, decision-making tends to be overly conservative because of fear of repercussions.  
- An environment that supports learning from errors encourages:  
  - Risk-taking.  
  - Innovation.  
  - Experimentation with new ideas.  
- Therefore, the post-mortem process is not just about fixing problems but also about enabling an organisation to **push boundaries and evolve**.

### Key Insights  
- **Mistakes are inevitable but valuable when used as learning tools**.  
- **Post-mortem reports document incidents to deeply understand causes and outcomes without blame**.  
- **Sharing post-mortems across teams cultivates organisational learning and improvement**.  
- **A culture that accepts mistakes fosters innovation and risk-taking**.

### Conclusion  
The video emphasises the vital role of post-mortem reports in transforming mistakes into organisational assets. By focusing on learning rather than blame, and promoting transparency through sharing, companies can develop resilient cultures that embrace innovation and continuous improvement. This approach mirrors the natural human learning process and is essential for healthy team dynamics and sustainable growth.
