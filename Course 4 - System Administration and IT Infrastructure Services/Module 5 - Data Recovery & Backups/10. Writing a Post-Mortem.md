
# [Writing a Post-Mortem](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D5eaCY86W8I&t=6s)

- 🔥 **Summary**
  – **Incident**: Outage occurred; brief description of what happened, including how it impacted services.
  – **Duration**: Length of the incident with explicit start and end times in a stated **time zone**.
  – **Impact**: What was affected (services, users, severity) and the overall effect.
  – **Resolution**: How the outage was fixed and restored, signaling the end of the event.

- ⏱ **Timeline**
  – A detailed sequence of events from incident start through notification, awareness, and actions taken to resolve the issue.
  – Includes timestamps with **dates, times, and time zones**, and identifies who performed each action.
  – Concludes with the final actions that resolved the outage and signaled service restoration.

- 🧭 **Root Cause**
  – Honest account of what led to the issue, without blame.
  – Examples provided: a **configuration change pushed live without proper testing** or a **typo in a command**.
  – Notes on contributing factors such as lack of testing or the need for automation, and how these relate to potential process improvements.

- 🛠 **Resolution & Recovery**
  – Detailed steps taken to recover, with **dates, times, and time zones** for each step.
  – For each action: rationale behind it and the outcome.
  – Provides context on how the steps collectively led to restoration of services.

- 🚀 **Actions for Improvement (Prevent Recurrence)**
  – List of specific actions to prevent similar incidents, including improvements to monitoring and visibility.
  – Highlights gaps discovered (e.g., visibility gaps, automation issues) and potential areas for enhancement.
  – Note that the postmortem should outline areas for improvement; does not prescribe concrete solutions but points to where work is needed and who may address it.

- ✅ **What Went Well**
  – Highlight fail-safe or fail-over systems that functioned as designed and mitigated outage severity.
  – Illustrates the tangible benefits of these systems and their justification in terms of cost and risk reduction.

- 📚 **Takeaways / Lessons Learned**
  – Emphasizes preparedness to learn from mistakes, owning them, and applying lessons to improve IT work and related processes.
  – Encourages ongoing improvement and accountability to reduce recurrence and improve response.
