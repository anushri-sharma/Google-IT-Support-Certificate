5. Magnetic tape backup media is best suited for:
✅ Long-term archival data

Tape is cost-effective for storing large amounts of data over long periods.

Magnetic tape backup media is best suited for long-term archival data.

While it might seem like "old" technology compared to modern Solid State Drives (SSDs),
magnetic tape remains a powerhouse in enterprise environments for several specific reasons:

- Longevity: Tape is incredibly stable. When stored in a controlled environment, magnetic
tapes can last for 30 years or more, whereas hard drives and SSDs have much shorter lifespans when left unpowered.

- Cost-Efficiency: On a per-gigabyte basis, tape is significantly cheaper for storing massive
 amounts of data (petabytes) than any other physical medium.

- Capacity: Modern LTO (Linear Tape-Open) cartridges can store massive amounts of compressed
 data in a very small physical footprint.

- Air-Gapping: Once a tape is removed from the drive, it is "air-gapped," meaning it is
physically disconnected from the network. This makes it immune to ransomware and cyberattacks.
