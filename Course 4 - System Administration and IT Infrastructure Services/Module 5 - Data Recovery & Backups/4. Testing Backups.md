Testing backups is a critical, yet often overlooked, aspect of data management.

Simply creating regular backups is insufficient; 

a well-defined and regularly tested recovery process is equally important. 

This process ensures that data can be restored effectively in the event of a data loss incident. 

Documentation of restoration procedures is essential, making them accessible to authorized personnel to 
facilitate quick recovery. **Disaster Recovery Testing (DRT)** is the practice of simulating disaster 
scenarios to evaluate an organization's preparedness. Conducting DRT annually, involving various teams 
like IT Support Specialists, helps identify weaknesses in emergency procedures and planning. 
These simulations provide a valuable opportunity to discover and rectify gaps in data protection 
strategies before a real-world data loss event occurs, thus improving the overall resilience of 
the recovery system.
