
# 10. [With Great Power Comes Great Responsibility](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6cM_R6nBH6o&t=6s)

🔥 **Avoid using administrative rights for tasks that don't require them**  
  – **Example:** do not browse the web as an administrator user.  
  – **Implication:** reduces attack surface and accidental system-wide changes.  
  – **Applies to all environments:** single machine, fleet, or cloud service with thousands of users.

⏱️ **Minimize time spent in an administrative session**  
  – **Practice:** do necessary admin actions, then close the session immediately.  
  – **Tool example:** in Linux, use **sudo** for temporary elevation rather than staying logged in as root.  
  – **Benefit:** limits window for mistakes or exploitation.

🔐 **Respect the privacy of others**  
  – **Principle:** having filesystem or server access does not justify reading private files or email.  
  – **Example:** filesystem access to a user's home directory or admin rights on an email server are not reasons to inspect personal data.  
  – **Process requirement:** if business reasons exist to access data, follow the appropriate policies and approval processes.

🧭 **Do not use admin rights to bypass roles or policies**  
  – **Rule:** avoid circumventing established role boundaries, approvals, or audit controls even when you can.  
  – **Implication:** bypassing undermines accountability and compliance.

📝 **Think before you type — plan and verify actions**  
  – **Risk examples:** deleting the wrong files, rebooting the wrong machine, or breaking the remote-management connection.  
  – **Mitigation:** write out the steps you plan to take before executing them to reduce errors.  
  – **Additional benefit:** pre-written steps double as documentation.

📚 **Document everything you do while elevated**  
  – **Why:** lists of executed commands enable repeatable procedures and troubleshooting of problems that arise later.  
  – **Tools:** **script** (Linux) records commands and output; **Start-Transcript** (Windows PowerShell) does equivalent; desktop-recording tools capture GUI interactions.  
  – **Secondary use:** captured logs can be used to automate the procedure in the future.

🔁 **Prepare a rollback plan before making changes**  
  – **Practice:** make copies of state before changing it (backups, snapshots, or export configurations).  
  – **Version control:** keep configurations in a **version control system** to simplify reverting.  
  – **Documentation:** record the exact steps required to return to the previous state.

⚖️ **Understand rollback difficulty and consequences**  
  – **Easy rollback example:** toggling a configuration setting from **true** to **false** can be reverted by resetting it to **true**.  
  – **Hard or impossible rollback example:** deleting a file without a backup may be unrecoverable.  
  – **Decision point:** before changing something, explicitly consider what the rollback would look like and whether you have necessary backups.

🛡️ **Recognize increased impact of administrator actions**  
  – **Principle (memorable phrase):** "with great power, comes great responsibility."  
  – **Consequence:** administrator actions can have much greater and broader effects than ordinary user actions.  
  – **Operational guidance:** minimize impact by reducing privilege use, documenting actions, and ensuring quick rollback capability.
