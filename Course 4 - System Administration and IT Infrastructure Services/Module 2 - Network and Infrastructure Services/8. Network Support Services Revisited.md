# Notes

 The video begins by introducing **network services commonly used within IT enterprise environments** to enhance employee productivity, privacy, and security. These services, although prevalent in larger organisations, might not be encountered in smaller businesses. Two primary examples discussed are **Intranets** and **proxy servers**.

- **Intranet**: Defined as an internal network exclusive to a company, accessible only to users connected to the company's network. The Intranet serves as a private platform akin to an internal company website.
- **Purpose of Intranet**: To improve productivity by enabling employees to share information efficiently within the organisation.

The Intranet provides multiple functionalities that support employee collaboration and information sharing:

- Centralised documentation repository.
- Platform for teams to post news updates.
- Facility for employees to complete forms.
- Space to initiate discussions.
  
**Intranets are predominantly utilised in large enterprises** and represent a valuable tool for fostering employee productivity by consolidating communication and information exchange in one secure location.

The discussion shifts to **proxy servers**, another key internal support service in IT environments:

- A proxy server acts as an **intermediary** between the company network and the Internet.
- It receives requests from internal users and relays them to the Internet on behalf of the company.
- This mechanism ensures that **company network traffic remains private from external Internet sources**, as the Internet only sees the proxy server’s address, not the original internal source.

Additional functionalities of proxy servers include:

- **Monitoring and logging internal network activity** to maintain oversight of employee Internet usage.
- **Filtering access to specific websites**, which can be configured to restrict access to unwanted or non-work-related content.

The proxy server thus serves a dual role of **enhancing privacy and security** and **regulating Internet access** within the organisation.
