🔥 **Peripherals = external devices that add functionality to a computer**  
  - **Examples:** mouse, keyboard, monitor, USB drives, external webcams, printers.  
  - **Definition:** any device connected externally to the computer.

💡 **USB is the most common peripheral connection (Universal Serial Bus)**  
  - **Common versions in modern systems:** **USB 2.0**, **USB 3.0**, **USB 3.1** (many systems may also show USB 3.2 or USB4).  
  - **Backward compatibility:** a **USB 2.0 device** will work in a **USB 3.x port** but operate at **USB 2.0 speeds**.

📊 **Units: Mb/s (megabit per second) vs MB (megabyte)**  
  - **Mb/s** = megabits per second, a unit of transfer rate; **MB** = megabytes, a unit of data size.  
  - **Conversion:** **1 byte = 8 bits**; to transfer **1 MB** in one second requires **8 Mb/s**.  
  - **Practical implication:** a listed speed of **40 Mb/s** does **not** equal 40 MB/s transfer; divide by 8 to convert to MB/s.

⚙️ **Typical USB speed references (context for performance expectations)**  
  - **USB 2.0:** **480 Mb/s** (0.48 Gb/s).  
  - **USB 3.0 (USB 3.1 Gen 1):** **5 Gb/s** (5000 Mb/s).  
  - **USB 3.1 Gen 2:** **10 Gb/s** (10000 Mb/s).  
  - **Implication:** using older ports or cables limits throughput regardless of device capability.

🎨 **Visual cues for USB ports (useful but not guaranteed)**  
  - **USB 2.0** ports often **black**.  
  - **USB 3.0** ports often **blue**.  
  - **USB 3.1** ports sometimes **teal**.  
  - **Caveat:** color coding **varies by manufacturer**—do not rely solely on color for capability.

🔌 **USB connector types and Type‑C specifics**  
  - **Multiple connector types** exist (A, B, micro, mini, Type-C, etc.); check supplemental specs for details.  
  - **USB Type‑C:** newest, reversible connector intended to **consolidate data, display, and power** functions; increasingly a universal standard.  
  - **Capabilities:** can carry **data**, **display signals** (via Alt Mode), and **power delivery**; functionality depends on the device, cable, and port implementation.

🖥️ **Display connections — video and audio considerations**  
  - **DVI:** generally carries **video only** (no audio); common on older monitors/projectors.  
  - **HDMI:** widely used; carries **both video and audio**; common on modern TVs and many computers.  
  - **DisplayPort:** carries **both video and audio**; widely used on modern monitors and GPUs.  
  - **USB‑C (with Alt Mode):** can carry **display + audio + data + power** when supported.

🔁 **Compatibility and practical choices for presentations and AV setups**  
  - For **video + audio** with a projector/monitor, prefer **HDMI** or **DisplayPort** (DVI may not provide audio).  
  - When connecting devices, ensure **port, cable, and device** support the required function (data speed, video, audio, power).  
  - **Adapters/cables** can bridge mismatched connectors but may limit features (e.g., using a DVI adapter vs HDMI).

💼 **IT support implication: know how to identify and match ports and capabilities**  
  - **Task frequency:** IT support frequently works with USB and display peripherals.  
  - **Skills needed:** distinguish port types, understand speed vs compatibility tradeoffs, choose appropriate cables/adapters, and verify whether a port supports display/audio/power.
