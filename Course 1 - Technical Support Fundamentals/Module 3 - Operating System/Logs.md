
[Logs](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fePKaGNZsvw)

Our computers actually can talk to us and tell us what's wrong.

Maybe they won't whisper answers to us, but they speak to us in the form of **logs**.

<img width="1271" height="422" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/9e3e9145-6cb2-4ab8-be2b-52c191dfa42b" />


**Logs or files that record system events on our computer, just like a system's diary**.

A computer will **record events** like when it was turned on, when a driver was loaded.

<img width="1280" height="668" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/9ea3e55e-767c-4369-b8cd-a4189d4885a2" />


And even when something isn't working in the form of **error messages**.

In all operating systems, logs are kept so we can refer back to them when we need to find out something that happened.

But logs can be hard to navigate because our computer will essentially record everything.
