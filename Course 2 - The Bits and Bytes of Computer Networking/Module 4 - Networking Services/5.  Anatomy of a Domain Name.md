
# [Anatomy of a Domain Name](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tqy65FgzHvY&t=12s)

🔥 **Domain names have three primary parts:** www, google, com  
  - **Example:** for www.google.com the three parts are **www**, **google**, and **com**.  
  - **Combining all parts** produces a **fully qualified domain name (FQDN)**.

🍳 **Top-Level Domain (TLD):** the last part (e.g., .com)  
  - **TLD examples:** **.com**, **.net**, **.edu**, country codes like **.de** (Germany) and **.cn** (China).  
  - **Vanity/new gTLDs:** newer options include **.museum**, **.pizza**, etc., expanding choices beyond the original set.  
  - **Governance:** **ICANN (Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers)** administers TLD policy and the global DNS; **IANA (Internet Assigned Numbers Authority)** performs assignment functions and is operated by ICANN.

💡 **Second-level domain (“domain”):** the middle part (e.g., google)  
  - **Purpose:** marks the boundary where control transfers from the TLD name server to an authoritative name server managed by the domain holder.  
  - **Registration:** can be chosen and registered by individuals or organizations but **must end in a predefined TLD**.  
  - **Cost:** **registering a domain costs money** through registrars.

🛰️ **Subdomain / Hostname:** the leftmost part (e.g., www)  
  - **Also called:** a subdomain or a **hostname** when assigned to a single host.  
  - **Assignment:** **subdomains are freely created and assigned** by whoever controls the registered domain (no separate registration fee to create them).  
  - **Examples:** you can have many levels such as host.sub.subdomain.domain.com, though long chains are uncommon.

🔧 **Registrars and how domain sales work:**  
  - **Registrar role:** ICANN‑accredited companies (registrars) sell unregistered domain names and manage registrations on behalf of customers.  
  - **Relationship:** registrars operate under agreements with ICANN to provide domain-registration services.

📐 **Technical limits and edge cases:**  
  - **Maximum labels/levels:** DNS supports up to **127 levels** (labels) in a single FQDN.  
  - **Label length:** each individual label (section between dots) can be at most **63 characters**.  
  - **Total length:** a complete FQDN is limited to **255 characters** (the conventional limit includes separators; the trailing root dot convention is part of FQDN syntax).
