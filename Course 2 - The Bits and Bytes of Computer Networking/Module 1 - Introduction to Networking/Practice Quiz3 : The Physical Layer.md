```
Half-Duplex: Think of a walkie-talkie. You have to press a button to speak, and
you can't hear the other person while you are talking. Only one person "owns" the line at a time.

Full-Duplex: Think of a smartphone call. Both people can talk and hear each other
simultaneously without waiting for the other to stop.
```


<img width="765" height="346" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/cfea7f9a-e7ad-4b0d-a1db-4de7dfcdf9a8" />

```
Twisted Pair: These are your standard Ethernet cables (like Cat5e or Cat6). They consist
of pairs of copper wires twisted together to reduce electromagnetic interference.

SATA: Used for connecting hard drives inside a computer.

VGA: Used for video output to older monitors.
```

```
Line Coding: This is the process of turning digital data into specific signals (like voltage levels) that can be sent over a physical wire.

RJ45: This is the name of the connector at the end of a twisted pair cable, not a modulation type.

Simplex: This is a communication direction (one-way only, like a TV broadcast).
```

These are all types of twisted pair Ethernet cables, categorized based on how they handle shielding to reduce interference:

UTP → No shielding
STP → Shielding around the wires
FTP → Foil shielding for added protection
