
# [Everything as a Service](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dSuNO9sUUh4&t=2s)

🔥 X as a Service (XaaS): cloud-era naming where “X” can be many things; the term signals the Cloud has grown beyond merely hosting virtual machines into layered, service-based offerings.  
  – **XaaS** general idea: outsource parts of IT stack so you pay a provider instead of building/maintaining that layer yourself.

🍳 **IaaS — Infrastructure as a Service**: abstracts away physical infrastructure (networks, servers, storage).  
  – You do not need to build or own hardware; you **pay a provider** to supply and manage the underlying infrastructure.  
  – Typical original interpretation of “the Cloud” aligns with **IaaS**.
  - **Infrastructure as a service abstracts** away the physical infrastructure you need

⚙️ **PaaS — Platform as a Service**: provides a managed execution environment or platform for running applications.  
  – Offers an **execution engine** and runtime environment so developers don’t need full server instances, complex filesystems, or dedicated resources.  
  – Focuses on “run the app” rather than “manage the server”; server details are abstracted away.
  - **Platform as a service abstracts** away the server instances you need.

🧩 **SaaS — Software as a Service**: centrally hosted, managed software offered under licensing/subscription rather than installed per machine.  
  – **SaaS** abstracts the software layer: provider handles hosting, updates, and operations while customers consume the application.  
  – Common model: **subscription-based** licensing and multi-tenant delivery.

📧 Concrete examples and candidates for SaaS:  
  – **Gmail for Business** and **Office 365 Outlook** are mainstream **SaaS** email examples where providers manage virtually everything.  
  – Modern web browsers enable many standalone apps to become SaaS: **word processors**, **graphic design tools**, **HR management systems**, etc.  
  – If an app can run in a browser, it’s a strong candidate for being offered as **SaaS**.

🌐 Implication for business networks and IT role:  
  – Increasingly the primary role of a business network is to provide **Internet access** to cloud-hosted software and data rather than host local applications.  
  – Shifts responsibility and operational burden to cloud providers across infrastructure, platform, or application layers depending on the service model.

🧭 Layering and relationships (summary of abstractions):  
  – **IaaS** abstracts hardware and physical infrastructure.  
  – **PaaS** abstracts server/instance and runtime environment (execution engine).  
  – **SaaS** abstracts application delivery, licensing, and ongoing management.
