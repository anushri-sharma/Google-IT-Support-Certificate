

# [Fiber Connections](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vdl_ADORgL8&t=4s)

🔥 **Fiber uses light for data transmission (not electrical currents)**  
  – **Light** allows signals to travel **much farther** and at **higher speeds** than electrical signals over copper.  
  – Copper electrical signals typically degrade after **thousands of feet** and need repeaters; fiber implementations can carry signals **many miles** before degradation.

💡 **Cost and historical deployment**  
  – Producing and laying **fiber is more expensive** than copper, so early use was limited to ISP core networks and data centers.  
  – Higher cost influenced network design choices and where fiber was placed in the network hierarchy.

📈 **Trend: fiber pushed closer to end users**  
  – In recent years fiber deployment has expanded from core networks toward the “last mile” to reach homes and businesses.  
  – How close fiber gets to the end user varies by implementation, prompting the FTTX naming scheme.

📦 **FTTX: “Fiber To The X” — a family of deployment models**  
  – **FTTX** denotes different termination points for fiber in the access network; the final segment may remain copper/coax depending on the model.  
  – The choice of X affects performance, cost, and required in-building/customer equipment.

🏘️ **FTTN — Fiber To The Neighborhood**  
  – Fiber terminates at a single physical cabinet serving part of a neighborhood.  
  – From that cabinet, **twisted-pair copper or coax** is typically used for the remaining distance to homes.

🏢 **FTTB — Fiber To The Building / Business / Basement**  
  – Fiber runs to an individual building or to a building’s entry/basement; can serve multi-tenant buildings or single businesses.  
  – Inside the building, **twisted-pair copper** is often used to connect individual units or offices.

🏠 **FTTH — Fiber To The Home (and FTTP — Fiber To The Premises)**  
  – **FTTH** places fiber all the way to each residence or apartment unit.  
  – **FTTH** and **FTTB** are often collectively called **FTTP (Fiber To The Premises)** when fiber reaches the customer premise.

🔌 **Demarcation: Optical Network Terminal (ONT)**  
  – Instead of a traditional modem, the demarcation point for fiber is the **Optical Network Terminal (ONT)**.  
  – The **ONT** converts signals/protocols used on the fiber network into the electrical protocols used by **twisted-pair copper** or customer equipment.
