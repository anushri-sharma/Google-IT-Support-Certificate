
# [Wireless Channels](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SszZ6aXyvEo&t=5s)

🔥 Collision domains and why channels matter  
  – **Collision domain:** a network segment where overlapping transmissions cause collisions; colliding devices must stop, wait a random time, then retry, which reduces throughput.  
  – **Wired mitigation:** switches reduce collisions by sending traffic only to the intended physical interface.  
  – **Wireless limitation:** no physical interfaces for each client, so devices share the same collision domain unless separated by frequency (channels).

💡 What a channel is and how it partitions spectrum  
  – **Channel:** a smaller frequency slice inside the overall wireless band used to separate transmissions.  
  – **2.4 GHz shorthand:** "2.4 GHz" refers roughly to **2.4–2.5 GHz**; channels subdivide that band.  
  – **Regulatory variance:** the number and selection of usable channels depend on country/region rules.

📐 802.11b example: channel width, midpoints, and overlap  
  – **Channel width:** **22 MHz** (for 802.11b), so a channel's signal spans beyond its center frequency and requires a frequency buffer.  
  – **Channel 1 midpoint:** **2412 MHz**, occupying about **2401–2423 MHz**.  
  – **Channel 6 midpoint:** **2437 MHz**; **Channel 11 midpoint:** **2462 MHz**.  
  – **Non-overlapping set:** in 802.11b, channels **1, 6, and 11** are spaced so they **do not overlap**; many other numbered channels do overlap and can interfere.

🔎 How access points choose channels and consequences  
  – **Auto-selection:** most APs can auto-sense channel congestion; some only on startup, others adjust dynamically.  
  – **Manual vs automatic:** manually set channels or AP auto-selection can still result in congestion or suboptimal choices.  
  – **Dense environments:** in urban areas with many networks, overlapping channels are common and degrade performance.

🛠️ Practical troubleshooting and deployment guidance  
  – **Use channel knowledge to troubleshoot** slow or unreliable Wi‑Fi by identifying and reducing overlapping channels.  
  – **Optimize deployments** by choosing channels that minimize overlap among your APs and those of neighbors.  
  – **Focus on concepts, not memorization:** understanding collision domains, channel width, and overlap is more important than memorizing every frequency number.

🌍 Signal behavior and edge cases  
  – **Radio imprecision:** transmissions spread beyond exact center frequencies, requiring channel width buffers that cause overlap.  
  – **Regional constraints:** available, legal channels and allowed power levels differ by jurisdiction, affecting planning and interference.
