🔥 **Multiple network models exist; course uses the _five-layer model_**  
  - Many variants appear in practice; some combine **physical** and **data link** into one layer, yielding a **four-layer** model.

💡 **OSI (open systems interconnection) model — a more rigorous seven-layer reference**  
  - The **OSI model** has **seven layers** and is often used in academic settings and by network certification organizations because it is more rigorously defined.

🧭 **OSI adds two layers between Transport and Application compared to the five-layer model**  
  - The OSI ordering (bottom→top) adds **Session (layer 5)** and **Presentation (layer 6)** between **Transport (layer 4)** and **Application (layer 7)**.

⚙️ **Session layer (OSI layer 5): role and behavior**  
  - Responsible for **facilitating communication between applications and the transport layer**.  
  - Acts in the operating system to take **unencapsulated application-layer data** (after lower-layer removal) and hand it to the Presentation layer.

<img width="1279" height="574" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/29e18559-b06c-486f-a76e-b0922ca32e30" />


🔐 **Presentation layer (OSI layer 6): translation and data-format functions**  
  - Ensures that the unencapsulated application data is **understandable by the receiving application**.  
  - May perform tasks such as **encryption** and **compression** to prepare data for the application.

<img width="1271" height="417" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/38bd9cea-2955-4d8c-b270-fd93f00cf9d0" />


🧩 **Encapsulation distinction and mapping to the five-layer model**  
  - The Session and Presentation layers operate on **unencapsulated** data (they do not perform additional encapsulation).  
  - For practical, day-to-day networking, those functions are **lumped into the Application layer** in the **five-layer model**.

📚 **Practical implication for learning and work**  
  - The **five-layer model** is considered most useful for everyday networking tasks.  
  - Understanding the **OSI seven-layer basics remains important** because the OSI model is prevalent in education and certification contexts.
