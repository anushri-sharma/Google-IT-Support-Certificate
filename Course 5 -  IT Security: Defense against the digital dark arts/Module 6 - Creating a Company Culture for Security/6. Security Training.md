
# [Security Training](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g_EjyPOQG0o&t=3s)

> 1. Importance of Security Training

- Well-trained employees are essential for strong organizational security.
- Lack of training leads to poor security practices and increased risk.
- Training improves overall company culture and awareness around security.

2. Building a Security-Focused Culture

- Encourage employees to speak up when something seems suspicious.
- Create an environment where asking questions about security is welcomed.
- Provide clear communication channels (e.g., mailing lists) for reporting concerns.

3. Benefits of Shared Responsibility

- When everyone is security-conscious:
- The burden on IT/support teams decreases.
- Overall organizational security improves.

4. Reinforcing Good Security Habits

- Security must be actively encouraged and rewarded.
- Small daily actions matter, such as:
    - Locking your screen when stepping away
    - Being cautious when entering passwords
    - Verifying website authenticity
    - Avoiding leaving devices unattended in public
 
5. Making Security Memorable

- Fun or memorable consequences (like harmless pranks) can reinforce habits.
- Example lesson: Not locking a computer can allow others to misuse access.

6. Need for Formal Training Programs

- Culture alone isn’t enough—structured training is necessary.
- Training can include:
    - Videos or presentations
    - Quizzes to test understanding and improve retention
- Regular retraining (e.g., annually) keeps employees updated.

7. Topics Covered in Security Training

- Common threats such as:
    - Phishing attacks
    - Password security best practices
- Scenario-based learning to improve real-world response.
