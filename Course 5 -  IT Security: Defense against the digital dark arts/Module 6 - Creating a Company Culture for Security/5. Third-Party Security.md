
# [Third-Party Security](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MNBNR8hIinQ&t=5s)

🔒 Need to use third parties and associated risks  
  – **Small IT shops** often must rely on third-party solutions or service providers when they cannot do everything in-house.  
  – Third parties may handle **sensitive data** or have **access to networks/systems**, so their security posture directly affects your defenses.  
  – A vendor with **subpar security** can create a new avenue of attack that undermines your organization’s security.

🧾 Vendor selection and controlled management  
  – Hire **trustworthy, reputable vendors** whenever possible.  
  – Manage engagements in a controlled manner by conducting a **vendor risk review** or **security assessment** before contracting.

🔍 Limitations of questionnaire-only assessments  
  – Typical assessments use a **questionnaire** covering security policies, procedures, and defenses to determine whether vendors have implemented good security designs.  
  – Questionnaires rely on **self-reporting**, which is inherently unreliable without independent verification.  
  – Whenever possible, **verify** questionnaire claims rather than assuming honesty.

🧾 Verify with independent evidence and test reports  
  – Request **third-party security assessment reports**, **audit results**, and **penetration testing reports** to corroborate questionnaire answers.  
  – For many items on a questionnaire, these documents provide verifiable evidence of security practices.

🧪 Software and hardware testing before contracting  
  – For **software services or hardware vendors**, perform or request testing of the product to evaluate potential security vulnerabilities.  
  – Basic **vulnerability assessments** and tests can show whether a product has reasonable security before committing.

🛠️ Onsite infrastructure: control, restrict, and monitor access  
  – If vendors install **infrastructure equipment on site**, verify that installation and management do not negatively affect overall security.  
  – If the vendor requires **remote access** to devices for maintenance, **adjust firewall rules** to restrict that access and prevent it from becoming a network entry point.  
  – Implement **additional monitoring** for third-party devices because they represent new potential attack surfaces.

🏷️ External evaluation services and free resources  
  – There are commercial firms that will **evaluate vendors for a fee**.  
  – **Google’s vendor security assessment questionnaires** are available for free and are a suitable starting point to design or use as your own questionnaire.

🧑‍🔬 Lab evaluation and remediation workflow  
  – If the vendor permits, evaluate hardware in a **lab environment** first to run in-depth vulnerability assessments and penetration tests without exposing production systems.  
  – **Report findings** to the vendor and require that they **address identified issues** before or as part of the engagement.

⚠️ Critical implication: vendor compromise can breach your systems  
  – When a third party handles data or has network access, the **security of your data and systems depends on the vendor’s security**.  
  – A compromise of vendor infrastructure can directly lead to a **breach of your systems**, so assess and mitigate that risk before contracting.
