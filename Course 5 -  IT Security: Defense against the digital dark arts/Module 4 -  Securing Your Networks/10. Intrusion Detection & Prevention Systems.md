 **Introduction to IDS/IPS Systems**  
Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS) and Intrusion Prevention Systems (IPS) function by **monitoring network traffic and analyzing it for malicious behavior**. IT support specialists may be responsible for supporting the platform running the IDS/IPS, maintaining system rules, and responding to alerts. The core role of IDS/IPS is to identify traffic exhibiting **characteristics indicative of malicious intent**.

- **IDS** is a **detection-only system**: it logs and alerts on suspicious traffic but does not take proactive blocking actions.
- **IPS** differs by being able to **actively block or drop malicious traffic** by adjusting firewall rules in real-time upon detection.

Both IDS and IPS can be deployed as either **host-based** or **network-based** systems.

**Differences Between IDS and IPS, and Deployment Types**  
- **Network Intrusion Detection System (NIDS)**: Deployed on a network segment or subnet to monitor all traffic passing through that segment.
- **Host-Based Intrusion Detection System (HIDS)**: Software installed on an individual host that monitors traffic to/from that host and may also monitor system files for unauthorized changes.

IDS/IPS vs. Firewalls:  
- Firewalls are designed to **prevent intrusions by blocking traffic at network boundaries** and enforce access controls between networks.
- NIDS focus on **detecting and alerting on malicious activity within the network**, which may not be visible to firewalls.
- Firewalls typically **only see traffic flowing between networks**, while NIDS can monitor **host-to-host communications inside the network**.

**Considerations for NIDS Placement and Traffic Visibility**  
- NIDS must be strategically placed within the **network topology** to have access to the traffic it needs to monitor.
- **Port mirroring (SPAN ports)** on enterprise switches is an effective method to capture traffic for analysis.
  - This feature can mirror all packets from a specific port, range of ports, or entire VLAN to another port.
- The NIDS host connects to this mirror port and enables **promiscuous mode** on the network interface to analyze all mirrored packets.
- A NIDS host requires **at least two network interfaces**:
  - One for **monitoring and analyzing mirrored traffic**.
  - Another for **management and administrative connectivity** to the wider network.

**NIPS Placement and Functionality**  
- Network Intrusion Prevention Systems (NIPS) must be placed **inline with the monitored traffic** to block or drop malicious packets effectively.
- Unlike passive NIDS, NIPS act as **active traffic controllers**, intervening by **blocking suspicious traffic** as it passes through.
- This inline placement is critical; without it, the NIPS cannot control or prevent detected threats.

**Threat Detection Methods and Maintenance Responsibilities**  
- IDS/IPS commonly rely on **signature-based detection**, similar to antivirus systems.
  - Signatures represent **unique patterns or sequences of packets** characteristic of known malicious activity.
  - These may include specific packet header fields or payload content.
- IT support specialists must ensure **rules and signatures are regularly updated** to maintain effective detection.
- Signature-based detection excels at identifying **known threats** such as botnets and worms but may miss **less common or targeted attacks** due to lack of existing signatures.
- Custom rules can be created to flag **suspicious but not definitively malicious traffic**, providing investigators an opportunity to analyze further.
- When confirmed malicious, new signatures can be developed and integrated into the system for future detection.

**Response Actions and Alerting in NIDS**  
- Upon detecting malicious traffic, the NIDS typically performs several actions:
  - **Logs the detection event** with a full packet capture for forensic analysis.
  - Sends an **alert notification to the investigation team**.
- Alert mechanisms vary based on severity:
  - Low severity may result in automated emails or ticket creation.
  - High severity alerts may trigger **immediate paging or escalation**, including out-of-hours notifications.
- Alerts usually include **reference information**:
  - Links to known vulnerabilities.
  - Additional context about the detected threat.
- This comprehensive alerting facilitates efficient and informed investigation by security personnel.

### Key Concepts Summary

| Term                     | Definition                                                                                  |
|--------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| IDS (Intrusion Detection System) | Monitors and alerts on malicious traffic but does not block it.                        |
| IPS (Intrusion Prevention System) | Monitors, alerts, and *actively blocks* malicious traffic inline.                      |
| NIDS (Network IDS)        | Monitors network segments by capturing traffic from switches or network taps.               |
| HIDS (Host IDS)           | Software on a host monitoring local traffic and system file integrity.                      |
| Port Mirroring (SPAN)     | Switch feature that duplicates network traffic to a monitoring port.                        |
| Signature-Based Detection | Uses known attack patterns to identify malicious traffic quickly and accurately.            |

### Differences Between IDS and IPS

| Feature                     | IDS                                    | IPS                                        |
|-----------------------------|---------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| Action on detection          | Alert and log only                    | Alert, log, and block/drop malicious traffic |
| Placement                   | Passive; monitors mirrored traffic   | Inline; must be in the path of the traffic |
| Impact on traffic flow       | None                                 | Can disrupt/block traffic                    |

### IDS/IPS Deployment Considerations

- **NIDS:** Requires network visibility via port mirroring or network taps; uses promiscuous mode on interfaces.
- **NIPS:** Must be inline to block malicious packets immediately.
- **Host-based systems:** Monitor specific hosts for traffic and file integrity.

### Responsibilities of IT Support Specialists

- Maintain underlying platform hosting IDS/IPS.
- Update rules and signatures regularly.
- Respond appropriately to alerts and incidents.
- Develop custom detection rules for suspicious traffic.
- Facilitate incident investigation with detailed logs and packet captures.

### Core Insights

- **IDS and IPS complement firewalls** by providing detailed detection within the network and the ability to block traffic in real-time (IPS).
- **Proper placement and configuration** of IDS/IPS systems are critical for effective network security monitoring.
- **Signature-based detection is effective for known threats but requires regular updates and custom rule development** to cover emerging or targeted attacks.
- **Alerting mechanisms must be tailored to the severity** of detected threats to ensure timely and effective response.
- **IT specialists play a crucial role in system upkeep, alert handling, and custom rule creation**, forming the backbone of intrusion detection/prevention efficacy.
