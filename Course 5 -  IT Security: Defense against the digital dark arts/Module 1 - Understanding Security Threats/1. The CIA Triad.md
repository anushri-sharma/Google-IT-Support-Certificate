
# [The CIA Triad](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T3veeu6iAIE&t=137s)


The CIA triad is a fundamental concept in information security, 
comprising Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability. 

<img width="1277" height="314" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/57635029-9615-43d5-bbdd-884aa8ca5841" />

**Confidentiality** ensures that data is kept hidden from unauthorized individuals, with password protection being a common example. 
Access should be limited to those who absolutely need it. 

<img width="1280" height="294" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/07cbf91c-0f56-4f4a-8687-2740fcaace51" />

**Integrity** means maintaining the accuracy and consistency of data, ensuring it remains untampered with throughout its journey. Any discrepancies, like a file size changing during download, indicate a potential security breach. 

<img width="1279" height="372" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/45c57050-0810-450c-bea5-0b3979b5f5de" />

**Availability** ensures that information is readily accessible to authorized users when needed. This includes being prepared for data loss or system downtime. Security attacks can compromise any of these principles, aiming to steal time, resources, or hold systems hostage. Understanding and implementing the CIA triad is crucial for developing effective security policies and protecting against such threats.
