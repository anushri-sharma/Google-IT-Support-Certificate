**Introduction to Malware and Its Types**  
- **Malware** is malicious software designed to steal sensitive information, delete, or modify files for unwanted purposes.  
- Common types of malware include:  
  - **Viruses**  
  - **Worms**  
  - **Adware**  
  - **Spyware**  
  - **Trojans**  
  - **Rootkits**  
  - **Backdoors**  
  - **Botnets**

**Viruses: Mechanism and Behavior**  
- Computer viruses mimic biological viruses:  
  - Biological viruses attach to healthy cells, replicate, and spread causing illness.  
  - Computer viruses attach to executable code (programs).  
- When the infected program runs, the virus spreads to multiple files, infecting them.  
- The virus replicates, performs malicious activities, and continues spreading until it reaches maximum infection.  
- **Key point:** Viruses require a host program to propagate and execute.

**Worms: Autonomous Malware Spread**  
- Worms differ from viruses as they do **not require a host program** to spread.  
- Worms can operate independently and propagate through networks.  
- **Example:** The **ILoveYou (Love Bug) worm**:  
  - Spread via email with subject line "I love you."  
  - Attached file disguised as a love letter text file but was actually executable malware.  
  - Once opened, it:  
    - Copied itself to multiple files and folders.  
    - Launched additional malicious software.  
    - Replaced legitimate files.  
    - Hid itself after execution.  
  - Spread by harvesting email addresses from victim's contacts and chat clients.  
  - Resulted in billions of dollars in global damage.  
- **Security lesson:** Never open email attachments from unknown or untrusted sources.

**Adware: Visible but Sometimes Malicious**  
- Adware primarily displays advertisements and collects user data.  
- It can be:  
  - **Legitimate:** Installed when users agree to terms for free software that shows ads.  
  - **Malicious:** Installed without consent, possibly performing harmful actions beyond ads.  
- Adware is one of the most commonly encountered malware types due to its visibility.

**Trojan Malware: The Digital Trojan Horse**  
- Named after the Greek myth of the Trojan Horse, where Greeks hid inside a wooden horse to infiltrate Troy.  
- **Trojan malware** disguises itself as legitimate software but performs malicious actions once installed.  
- Important aspects:  
  - Requires **user execution**; it must be willingly run by the user.  
  - Uses deception to entice users to install it, often appearing as trusted software.  
- No malware installs itself without some form of user consent or action in the case of Trojans.

**Spyware: Surveillance Malware**  
- Designed to **monitor user activity** without consent.  
- Common spyware functionalities include:  
  - Monitoring computer screens.  
  - Recording keystrokes.  
  - Accessing webcams.  
  - Streaming or sending captured data to a third party.

- **Keylogger:** A notable type of spyware that records every keystroke, capturing messages, passwords, and sensitive inputs.

**Ransomware: Hostage Malware and High-Profile Case**  
- Ransomware attacks hold data or entire systems hostage until a ransom is paid.  
- **Case study: WannaCry ransomware attack (May 2017):**  
  - Exploited vulnerability in outdated Windows systems.  
  - Infected hundreds of thousands of machines worldwide.  
  - Especially severe impact on the UK's National Health Service (NHS), causing system shutdowns and a health crisis.  
- **Insight:** Ransomware attacks are increasing in frequency and severity, requiring preparedness and defensive measures.

| Virus         | Attaches to executable programs, spreads via infected files | Requires host program; replicates and spreads on execution | General concept explained  |

---

### Key Insights  
- **Malware can take various forms**, each with distinct propagation and impact mechanisms.  
- **Viruses and worms differ primarily in dependency on host programs**: viruses need hosts, worms do not.  
- **User interaction is critical for Trojans to infiltrate systems.**  
- **Adware may be legal or malicious; user awareness of installation consent is vital.**  
- **Spyware poses serious privacy risks by covertly monitoring user activity.**  
- **Ransomware causes severe operational disruptions and financial damage, exemplified by WannaCry.**  
- **Email remains a significant vector for malware transmission, highlighting the importance of cautious attachment handling.**  

---

### Core Concepts  
- **Replication and Spread:** Viruses and worms propagate by replicating themselves, but worms can spread without attaching to a program.  
- **Deception:** Trojans use disguise and social engineering to trick users into installing malware.  
- **Surveillance:** Spyware covertly collects data, often without the user’s knowledge.  
- **Hostage Situations:** Ransomware encrypts or locks data, demanding payment for restoration.  

---

### Recommendations Highlighted  
- Avoid opening **unrecognized email attachments** to prevent worm infections.  
- Be cautious about software installation to avoid **Trojan malware**.  
- Understand terms of service when agreeing to free software with **adware**.  
- Maintain updated systems to reduce vulnerability to attacks like **WannaCry ransomware**.
